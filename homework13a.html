More on the oddball र: the vowel ऋ and the ृ matra

Points to remember:

  1. The ृ is a matra, and all matras are associated with vowels. This matra is associated with the vowel ऋ - Hindi speakers pronounce this vowel exactly like you would pronounce रि.
  2. An interesting point (especially for parents) is that this letter is supposed to be pronounced very differently in Sanskrit - my research tells me that to say ऋ you have to roll your tongue and say a long rrrr like you were saying the English word work, stretching out the r a bit: worrrrrk.
  3. We will stick to the रि pronunciation of ऋ as that is how the Hindi experts pronounce it.
  4. The ृ matra is written by writing a small C-shaped hook at the bottom of the letter. Examples : कृ पृ टृ नृ तृ जृ सृ
  5. For the letter ह, this is how you add the matra: हृ.
  6. For the letter द, I have always written it with a hook below the letter, but this is how it shows up on the computer: दृ (you write only half the द, and then add the hook.)
  7. The ृ matra is similarly pronounced: if you see a कृ, say it exactly like you would say क्रि. पृ is said like प्रि.

Letter recognition practice: प फ फ़ ब भ म. Note the difference between फ and फ़ (ph and f).

Note: The फ़ sound came to us from the Persian language (it did not exist in Sanskrit). All words in Hindi which contain the फ़ sound SHOULD be written with a dot under the फ, but in reality, even the Hindi textbook writers don't enforce that. Today, it seems to be quite acceptable to write the फ़ as a फ (without the dot) - nobody seems to notice the difference - almost everybody pronounces the word as they think it should sound like (sometimes with a ph sound, more often with a f sound) - irrespective of the fact that there is (or isn't) a dot there. For these exercises, however, make the difference obvious when you say फ़ and फ.
