नाम: --------------------------------------------------

New letters:
the viram (विराम) or halant (हलन्त) : ्,
the visarg (विसर्ग): ः,
ॐ,
ज्ञ

Points to remember:

  1. The halant (्) is not seen too often in Hindi. It is sometimes (rarely) used to write half letters. For example: instead of writing चप्पल, I can write it as चप्‌पल. Most Hindi words which do not end in a matra actually end in a halant: काम = काम्, चुप = चुप्. Some words are impossible to pronounce with an ending halant: example: आदित्य, कृष्ण, इंद्र

    In Sanskrit (NOT in Hindi), any letter without a halant is pronounced fully. Sanskrit, thus, has no ambiguities on how to pronounce a certain word.

    In Sanksrit, भावना is pronounced exactly the way it is written: भा + व + ना.

    In Hindi, the same word is written the same way: भावना but usually pronounced भाव्‌ना or भाव्ना or भा + व् + ना

    Original Sanksrit words like शिव, योग, अशोक are pronounced in Hindi as शिव्, योग्, अशोक्. In an attempt to save the full letter sound of the last letter in each of those words, they are written in English as Shiva, Yoga, Ashoka, but now their pronunciation becomes शिवा, योगा, अशोका.

  2. The pronunciation of the विसर्ग is ह or हा or हि... depending on the matra that preceeds it. This is not too common in Hindi, but there are a handful of commonly used words that do have it.
  3. ॐ is pronounced ओम् is considered a sacred symbol and sound in Hinduism. Note the difference between ॐ and ऊँ. Note that ॐ is never written with a line on top, and it looks a lot fancier.
  4. ज्ञ, in Hindi, has the same sound as ग्य (experts say it is quite different in Sanskrit but we will not go into that). ज्ञान = ग्यान, आज्ञा = आग्या, ...
  5. ज़(z), like फ़, comes to us from Persian. It did not exist in Sanskrit.
