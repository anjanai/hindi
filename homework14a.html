New letters: श, ष, श्र, क्ष

Points to remember:

  1. The श and ष are supposed to be pronounced differently, but hardly any Hindi speaker does that.
    Ideally, you should pronounce the ष with your tongue rolled back.
  2. The half letter form of श can be a little different depending on the letter that follows it.
    You should be able to recognize both forms: The first form is what you would expect - examples: श्क श्म श्य.
    The second form looks like this: श्च (च) श्न (न) श्ल (ल) श्व (व).
  3. And, of course, Mr. ODDBALL र has to be different!
    Remember how half त combined with र to give us a completely new letter त्र?
    Something similar happens for श: we have a new letter: श्र.
  4. Another very odd thing concerns the combination of श, र, and the ृ matra...
    There are many Sanskrit words - which can be spelt two ways in Hindi - both of which seem to be accepted.
    Logically, these should have had the combination श + ृ , but they are more commonly
    written with these three combined : श, र, ृ to give us: श्रृ.
    Logically speaking, there is no business for the letter combination श्रृ to exist.
    Hindi DOES NOT have any combinations like म्रृ क्रृ न्रृ - but for some reason,
    the श्रृ combination is seen more often than the शृ combination.
    Where it exists, you should pronounce it like you would शृ.
    In all such words, श + र + ृ does not have the sound sh + ra + ri, but the sound sh + ri.
    Remember that श्रृ has the same sound as शृ : sh + ri.
  5. The only oddity of ष is when it combines with half क to give us क्ष.
